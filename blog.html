My First Sloth Sighting

It was a slow, humid morning in Manuel Antonio National Park when I spotted my first sloth. High in the canopy, a three-toed sloth was slowly making its way across a branch. I was mesmerized. Its gentle movements and serene face were a stark contrast to the lively monkeys and birds all around it. It was a moment of pure, unhurried bliss.

The Truth About "Sloth Rescues"

While researching for this blog, I came across several organizations claiming to "rescue" sloths. Sadly, many of these are nothing more than tourist traps, using sloths as photo props. A truly ethical rescue center focuses on rehabilitation and releasing sloths back into the wild, not on human interaction. I'll be dedicating a future post to identifying legitimate organizations.

August 10, 2025

Sloths in Pop Culture

From the cheerful Flash in *Zootopia* to the recent documentaries highlighting their survival struggles, sloths have captured the public's imagination. It's great to see these creatures get the attention they deserve, but it's important to remember that they are wild animals, not pets. Their slow nature is a survival strategy, not a character trait for our amusement!
